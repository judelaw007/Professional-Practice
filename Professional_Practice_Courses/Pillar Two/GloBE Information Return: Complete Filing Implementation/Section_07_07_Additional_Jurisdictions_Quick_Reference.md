# Section 7.7: Additional Jurisdictions Quick Reference

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this section, you will be able to:
- Identify key filing requirements across major implementing jurisdictions
- Access the relevant tax authority portals for each jurisdiction
- Understand jurisdiction-specific deadlines and penalties
- Apply the Jurisdiction Comparison Matrix for multi-jurisdiction filing coordination

## Introduction

This section provides quick reference guidance for additional jurisdictions implementing Pillar Two. For each jurisdiction, we cover the essential filing information practitioners need: portal access, deadlines, key requirements, and penalties.

**Note:** Pillar Two implementation is evolving. Always verify current requirements with the relevant tax authority before filing.

## 7.7.1 France

### Overview

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Legislation** | Loi de Finances 2024 (Article 33) |
| **Tax Authority** | Direction Générale des Finances Publiques (DGFiP) |
| **Portal** | impots.gouv.fr |
| **IIR/QDMTT Effective** | FY starting on/after 1 January 2024 |
| **UTPR Effective** | FY starting on/after 1 January 2025 |
| **QDMTT Status** | Qualified (OECD Central Record January 2025) |

### Filing Requirements

```
France Filing Obligations:
│
├── FORM 2065-INT-SD
│   ├── Part of corporate income tax return
│   ├── CbCR and Pillar Two notification combined
│   ├── Due with CIT return (May for calendar year)
│   └── Available on impots.gouv.fr
│
├── GloBE INFORMATION RETURN
│   ├── Deadline: 15 months (18 months first year)
│   ├── First deadline: 30 June 2026 (FY 2024)
│   └── Filed per Articles 223 WW / 223 WW bis
│
└── GloBE TAX RETURN
    ├── Same deadline as GIR
    ├── Self-assessment of top-up tax
    └── Payment with return
```

### Key Deadlines

| Filing | Standard | First Year | First Due Date |
|--------|----------|------------|----------------|
| Form 2065-INT-SD | May (with CIT) | May 2025 | 19 May 2025 (CY) |
| GIR | 15 months | 18 months | 30 June 2026 |
| Tax Return | 15 months | 18 months | 30 June 2026 |

### Simplified Transitional Reporting

France allows simplified reporting (per Decree 2024-1126) when:
- Fiscal year began before 31 December 2028 and ends by 30 June 2030
- No top-up tax requiring apportionment is payable

### Portal Information

| Resource | URL |
|----------|-----|
| Tax Portal | https://www.impots.gouv.fr |
| Professional Space | https://cfspro.impots.gouv.fr |
| Form 2065-INT-SD | Available on impots.gouv.fr |

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Legislation** | Federal Act on the Implementation of the OECD/G20 Minimum Tax |
| **Tax Authority** | Swiss Federal Tax Administration (SFTA) |
| **Portal** | OMTax (via ePortal) |
| **QDMTT Effective** | FY starting on/after 1 January 2024 |
| **IIR Effective** | FY starting on/after 1 January 2025 |
| **UTPR Effective** | To be determined |

### Filing Requirements

```
Switzerland Filing Obligations:
│
├── REGISTRATION (via OMTax)
│   ├── Available from 1 January 2025
│   ├── Must complete before filing tax return
│   ├── Deadline: Before first return (typically 30 June 2026)
│   ├── Required even for groups using CbCR safe harbour
│   └── Available in DE, FR, IT, EN
│
├── SWISS QDMTT RETURN
│   ├── Filed via OMTax
│   ├── Deadline: 18 months after first in-scope FY end
│   ├── First deadline: 30 June 2026 (FY 2024)
│   └── One taxable entity per MNE group in Switzerland
│
└── GloBE INFORMATION RETURN
    ├── Filed via OMTax
    ├── Same deadline as QDMTT Return
    └── 30 June 2026 first deadline
```

### Key Deadlines

| Filing | Standard | First Year | First Due Date |
|--------|----------|------------|----------------|
| Registration | Before filing | Before filing | Before 30 June 2026 |
| QDMTT Return | 15 months | 18 months | 30 June 2026 |
| GIR | 15 months | 18 months | 30 June 2026 |

### Transitional CbCR Safe Harbour

Switzerland applies transitional safe harbour with graduated ETR thresholds:
- FY 2024-2025: 15%
- FY 2026: 16%
- FY 2027-2028: 17%

## Summary

This quick reference guide covers the essential filing information for major Pillar Two implementing jurisdictions. Key observations:

1. **Common Deadline:** 15 months (18 months first year) is the universal GIR deadline
2. **First Filings:** Most due 30 June 2026 for calendar year-end groups
3. **Portal Variation:** Each jurisdiction has unique portal requirements
4. **Registration:** Many jurisdictions require advance registration
5. **Penalties:** Vary significantly by jurisdiction

**Best Practice:** Always verify current requirements directly with the relevant tax authority, as Pillar Two implementation continues to evolve.
