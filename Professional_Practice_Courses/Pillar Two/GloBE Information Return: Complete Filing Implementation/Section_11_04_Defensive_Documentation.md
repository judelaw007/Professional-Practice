# Section 11.4: Defensive Documentation

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this section, you will be able to:
- Identify what documentation must be retained for GIR compliance
- Apply jurisdiction-specific retention period requirements
- Build a comprehensive audit-ready file structure
- Use the GIR Audit File Index to organise documentation
- Demonstrate "reasonable measures" through contemporaneous records

---

## Introduction

Effective documentation serves multiple critical purposes in Pillar Two compliance:

1. **Penalty mitigation**: Demonstrating "reasonable measures" under the OECD Transitional Penalty Relief regime
2. **Audit defence**: Providing evidence to support GIR positions during tax authority examinations
3. **Internal governance**: Ensuring accountability and oversight of GloBE calculations
4. **Cross-border consistency**: Maintaining aligned positions across multiple jurisdictions
5. **Amendment support**: Enabling accurate correction when errors are identified

This section establishes a comprehensive documentation framework that satisfies requirements across all implementing jurisdictions.

### 1.2 Supporting Documentation Standards

**Contemporaneous Documentation:**
Documentation should be prepared at the time calculations are performed, not reconstructed after authority enquiry.

**Version Control:**
Maintain clear version history showing:
- Document creation date
- Author identification
- Revision history
- Final/locked status

**Cross-Referencing:**
Each document should reference:
- Source systems/documents
- Related workpapers
- GIR data elements supported

## Part 2: Retention Periods by Jurisdiction

### 2.1 Statutory Retention Requirements

| Jurisdiction | General Tax Records | GloBE-Specific | Election Records | Notes |
|--------------|---------------------|----------------|------------------|-------|
| **United Kingdom** | 6 years | 6 years minimum | Life of election + 6 years | Para 46, Schedule 14 |
| **Germany** | 10 years | 10 years | 10 years | AO §147 |
| **France** | 6 years | 6 years | Life + 6 years | LPF |
| **Ireland** | 6 years | 6 years | Life + 6 years | TCA 1997 |
| **Netherlands** | 7 years | 7 years | 7 years | AWR |
| **Luxembourg** | 10 years | 10 years | 10 years | AO Luxembourg |
| **Australia** | 5 years | 5 years (potentially longer) | Life + 5 years | TAA 1953 |
| **Switzerland** | 10 years | 10 years | 10 years | OR |
| **Japan** | 7 years | 7 years | 7 years | NTA requirements |
| **Singapore** | 5 years | 5 years (expected) | Life + 5 years | IRAS |
| **Belgium** | 7 years | 7 years | 7 years | CIR |
| **Italy** | 10 years | 10 years | 10 years | Civil Code |
| **Spain** | 4 years (+2 if losses) | 6 years | Life + 4 years | LGT |
| **Sweden** | 7 years | 7 years | 7 years | BFL |
| **South Korea** | 5-10 years | 10 years | 10 years | NTA |

### 2.2 Practical Retention Recommendation

**Recommended Standard: 10 Years Minimum**

Rationale:
- Covers longest statutory period (Germany, Luxembourg, Italy)
- Accommodates extended assessment periods for fraud/negligence
- Supports five-year election consistency verification
- Allows for amended return preparation
- Enables cross-border coordination

**Extended Retention Cases:**

| Situation | Recommended Retention |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Ongoing dispute | Until resolution + 3 years |
| Elections with future impact | Life of election + 10 years |
| Positions under challenge | Until final determination + 6 years |
| Fraud/negligence risk | 20 years |
| Cross-border positions | 10 years minimum |

### 3.2 Document Quality Standards

**Minimum Standards for All Documents:**

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Header | Document title, version, date, author |
| Purpose | Clear statement of document objective |
| Source references | Link to underlying data/systems |
| Calculations | Show all formulas, not just results |
| Assumptions | Document all assumptions made |
| Conclusions | Clear statement of position taken |
| Sign-off | Preparer, reviewer, approver signatures |

**Calculation Workpaper Standards:**

```
Workpaper Header:
─────────────────────────────────────────────
Company:        [MNE Group Name]
Fiscal Year:    [FY20XX]
Jurisdiction:   [Jurisdiction]
Subject:        [Calculation description]
Prepared by:    [Name]          Date: [Date]
Reviewed by:    [Name]          Date: [Date]
Approved by:    [Name]          Date: [Date]
Version:        [X.X]
Status:         [Draft/Final/Filed]
─────────────────────────────────────────────

Contents:
1. Purpose
2. Data sources
3. Methodology
4. Calculations
5. Reconciliation
6. Conclusion
7. Appendices
```

### 3.3 Evidence of "Reasonable Measures"

To support transitional penalty relief claims, document:

**Process Evidence:**

| Evidence | Document |
|----------|----------|
| Professional advice obtained | Engagement letters, advisor emails |
| Systems implemented | System specifications, go-live records |
| Controls established | Control matrix, testing evidence |
| Training completed | Attendance records, materials |
| Reviews performed | Sign-off sheets, review notes |
| Timelines met | Project plans, milestone tracking |

**Good Faith Evidence:**

| Evidence | Document |
|----------|----------|
| Early preparation | Project initiation records |
| Resource allocation | Budget approvals, staffing records |
| Proactive authority engagement | Registration confirmations |
| Error identification process | Internal audit reports |
| Prompt correction | Amendment filing records |
| Continuous improvement | Process update documentation |

## Key Takeaways

1. **Contemporaneous documentation**: Prepare records at time of calculation, not after authority enquiry

2. **10-year retention standard**: Apply the longest statutory requirement across all jurisdictions

3. **Structured audit file**: Use consistent folder structure enabling rapid response to queries

4. **Evidence of reasonable measures**: Critical for transitional penalty relief claims

5. **Cross-referencing**: Every document should link to source data and GIR elements

6. **Version control**: Maintain clear audit trail of document evolution

7. **Sign-off discipline**: Three-level approval (preparer, reviewer, approver) for all key documents

8. **Reconciliation focus**: GIR-to-CbCR and GIR-to-FS reconciliations are authority priority areas
