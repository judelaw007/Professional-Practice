# Case Study 5: Phoenix Aerospace Holdings - Amendment Required

## Scenario Context

This case study presents **Phoenix Aerospace Holdings plc**, a UK-parented multinational that discovers a material error in its filed GIR. You will practice understanding the amendment process, penalty mitigation strategies, and documentation requirements.

## Learning Objectives

By completing this case study, you will be able to:
- Identify material errors requiring GIR amendment
- Apply materiality assessment frameworks
- Understand UK amendment procedures and time limits
- Apply transitional penalty relief provisions
- Prepare amendment documentation

| Attribute | Detail |

| **Original GIR Filed** | 15 May 2026 |

## The Error

During post-filing internal audit (3 September 2026), an error was discovered in the Irish jurisdictional calculation:

**Nature of Error:**
- Intercompany royalty income of €14.2 million incorrectly excluded from Irish GloBE Income
- Associated Irish covered taxes of €1.59 million correspondingly omitted

**Error Classification:** Category 4 - Omission Error (highest risk category)

## Key Concepts

### Materiality Tests
- Absolute change >€100,000?
- Percentage change >10%?
- Impact on ETR >0.5%?

### Transitional Penalty Relief
The OECD transitional period (FY2024 within scope) provides relief where MNE demonstrates "reasonable measures" were taken.
