# Section 13.4: National Implementation Guidance

## PART 4: ESSENTIAL RESOURCES

### Official Resource Directory

## Introduction

This section provides a comprehensive index of national implementation guidance, technical manuals, and interpretive documents issued by tax authorities across key jurisdictions implementing Pillar Two. Each entry includes direct links, publication dates, and summaries of key content.

> **Purpose:** National implementation guidance interprets how OECD Model Rules apply within each jurisdiction's domestic framework. This guidance is essential for understanding local variations, filing requirements, and compliance expectations.

| **FAQ** | https://www.bzst.de/EN/Businesses/Pillar_2/FAQ/faq_node.html |

**German-Specific Concepts:**

**Minimum Tax Group (Mindeststeuergruppe):**
Germany introduced a unique "Minimum Tax Group" concept that aggregates all top-up taxes (IIR, UTPR, QDMTT) at the level of a single entity—the Minimum Tax Group Head. This is specific to Germany and not covered by OECD Model Rules.

**Filing Requirements:**

| Obligation | Deadline | Filing Method |
|------------|----------|---------------|
| **Group Head Notification** | 28 February 2025 (calendar year) | BZSt online portal |
| **GIR** | 30 June 2026 (first year, 18 months) | Electronic via BZSt |
| **Minimum Tax Return** | 30 June 2026 | State tax office |

**2025 Legislative Updates:**
- Amendments implementing OECD Administrative Guidance (December 2023, May 2024, January 2025)
- DAC9 legislation for GIR exchange
- Simplification measures for anti-profit shifting rules

**Qualified Status:**
- IIR: Qualified (OECD Central Record)
- DMTT: Qualified (OECD Central Record)
- QDMTT Safe Harbour: Yes
