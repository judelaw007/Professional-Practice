# Section 8.2: Eligibility Assessment

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this section, you will be able to:
- Apply the DFE eligibility criteria to constituent entities
- Evaluate single versus multiple DFE structures
- Analyse group structure implications for DFE selection
- Use the DFE Eligibility Flowchart for systematic assessment
- Document eligibility determination with supporting evidence

## Introduction

Not every constituent entity can serve as a Designated Filing Entity. Specific eligibility criteria must be met, and certain group structures create complexities that require careful analysis. This section provides a systematic approach to assessing DFE eligibility and selecting the optimal structure.

---

## 8.2.1 Core Eligibility Criteria

### Fundamental Requirements

For an entity to be eligible as a DFE, it must meet all of the following criteria:

```
DFE Core Eligibility Criteria:
│
├── CRITERION 1: Constituent Entity Status
│   │
│   ├── Must be a Constituent Entity of the MNE group
│   ├── Included in consolidated financial statements
│   ├── Part of the group for GloBE purposes
│   └── Not a Joint Venture or JV subsidiary (special rules apply)
│
├── CRITERION 2: Located in Implementing Jurisdiction
│   │
│   ├── Entity must be tax resident in a jurisdiction that:
│   │   ├── Has implemented Pillar Two domestically
│   │   ├── Has GIR filing mechanisms in place
│   │   └── Accepts DFE filings (some jurisdictions require UPE)
│   │
│   └── Examples of implementing jurisdictions:
│       UK, Germany, Netherlands, France, Ireland, Australia,
│       Singapore, Japan, South Korea, Canada, etc.
│
├── CRITERION 3: Not an Excluded Entity
│   │
│   ├── Entity must NOT be an Excluded Entity under GloBE Rules:
│   │   ├── Governmental Entity
│   │   ├── International Organisation
│   │   ├── Non-profit Organisation
│   │   ├── Pension Fund
│   │   └── Investment Fund (that is a UPE)
│   │
│   └── Excluded Entities cannot fulfil filing obligations
│
├── CRITERION 4: Exchange Agreement Coverage
│   │
│   ├── Jurisdiction should have exchange agreements (QCAAs)
│   │   with other jurisdictions where group operates
│   ├── Enables central filing to satisfy other jurisdictions
│   ├── Without exchange, local filings still required
│   └── Check GIR MCAA signatory status
│
└── CRITERION 5: Practical Capacity
    │
    ├── Entity must have capacity to:
    │   ├── Collect data from all constituent entities
    │   ├── Prepare compliant GIR
    │   ├── File with local tax authority
    │   └── Respond to queries and manage amendments
    │
    └── May be satisfied through group support or advisers
```

## 8.2.2 Jurisdiction-Specific Requirements

### Variations by Jurisdiction

Different jurisdictions have varying requirements for DFE acceptance:

```
Jurisdiction-Specific DFE Requirements:
│
├── UNITED KINGDOM
│   ├── DFE concept: Recognised under UK rules
│   ├── Registration: Via Report Pillar 2 top-up taxes service
│   ├── Exchange: GIR MCAA signatory
│   └── Notes: UK entity can be DFE for foreign-parented groups
│
├── GERMANY
│   ├── DFE concept: Group Head (Gruppenträger) similar
│   ├── Registration: Via BZSt portal
│   ├── Exchange: GIR MCAA signatory
│   └── Notes: Specific Group Head notification required
│
├── NETHERLANDS
│   ├── DFE concept: Designated filing entity recognised
│   ├── Registration: Via Mijn Belastingdienst Zakelijk
│   ├── Exchange: GIR MCAA signatory
│   └── Notes: Can accept DFE filings
│
├── IRELAND
│   ├── DFE concept: Recognised under Irish rules
│   ├── Registration: Via ROS (Pillar Two Hub)
│   ├── Exchange: GIR MCAA signatory
│   └── Notes: Common choice for US-parented groups
│
├── SINGAPORE
│   ├── DFE concept: Designated Local GIR Filing Entity (GFE)
│   ├── Registration: Via myTax Portal (Corppass)
│   ├── Exchange: Expected GIR MCAA participation
│   └── Notes: Local GFE designation required regardless
│
├── AUSTRALIA
│   ├── DFE concept: Recognised under Australian rules
│   ├── Registration: Via ATO Online Services
│   ├── Exchange: GIR MCAA participation
│   └── Notes: Can file GIR centrally or receive via exchange
│
└── OTHER JURISDICTIONS
    └── Check local implementation for DFE acceptance rules
```

### Exchange Agreement Verification

```
Verifying Exchange Agreement Coverage:
│
├── STEP 1: Identify DFE Jurisdiction
│   └── Where is the proposed DFE located?
│
├── STEP 2: List All Group Jurisdictions
│   └── Where does the MNE group have constituent entities?
│
├── STEP 3: Check GIR MCAA Status
│   ├── Is DFE jurisdiction a GIR MCAA signatory?
│   ├── Are other group jurisdictions GIR MCAA signatories?
│   └── Source: OECD website (updated periodically)
│
├── STEP 4: Assess Exchange Coverage
│   │
│   ├── FULL COVERAGE
│   │   ├── All jurisdictions participate in GIR MCAA
│   │   ├── Central filing will work
│   │   └── Local notifications only required
│   │
│   ├── PARTIAL COVERAGE
│   │   ├── Some jurisdictions not in GIR MCAA
│   │   ├── Central filing for participating jurisdictions
│   │   └── Separate filings needed for non-participating
│   │
│   └── NO COVERAGE
│       ├── DFE jurisdiction not in GIR MCAA
│       ├── Central filing will not satisfy other jurisdictions
│       └── Consider alternative DFE jurisdiction
│
└── STEP 5: Document Coverage Assessment
    └── Record which jurisdictions covered/not covered
```

## 8.2.3 Single vs. Multiple DFE Structures

### Single DFE Approach (Recommended)

```
Single DFE Structure:
│
├── CHARACTERISTICS
│   ├── One entity designated as DFE for entire group
│   ├── Single GIR filed globally
│   ├── Exchanged to all other jurisdictions
│   └── Maximum simplification
│
├── ADVANTAGES
│   ├── Single point of accountability
│   ├── Consistent data across all filings
│   ├── Simplified coordination
│   ├── Reduced compliance costs
│   ├── Clear governance structure
│   └── Easier audit trail
│
├── DISADVANTAGES
│   ├── Single point of failure risk
│   ├── DFE must coordinate with all CEs
│   ├── May have limited local expertise
│   └── Time zone challenges for global groups
│
├── BEST FOR
│   ├── Groups with strong central tax function
│   ├── Groups with good data infrastructure
│   ├── Groups where one jurisdiction dominates
│   └── Most MNE groups (default recommendation)
│
└── STRUCTURE DIAGRAM

    ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                       UPE                           │
    │                  (Non-Implementing)                 │
    │                        │                            │
    │            ┌───────────┼───────────┐                │
    │            ▼           ▼           ▼                │
    │      ┌─────────┐  ┌─────────┐  ┌─────────┐          │
    │      │ DFE     │  │ CE 2    │  │ CE 3    │          │
    │      │(Files   │  │(Notify) │  │(Notify) │          │
    │      │ GIR)    │  │         │  │         │          │
    │      └─────────┘  └─────────┘  └─────────┘          │
    │           │                                         │
    │           └──── GIR exchanged to all ────────────►  │
    └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Multiple DFE Approach (Complex)

```
Multiple DFE Structure:
│
├── CHARACTERISTICS
│   ├── More than one entity designated as DFE
│   ├── May be regional or functional split
│   ├── Each DFE files for portion of group
│   └── Coordination between DFEs required
│
├── WHEN CONSIDERED
│   ├── Very large, complex group structures
│   ├── Distinct regional operations (EMEA, APAC, Americas)
│   ├── Regulatory requirements in certain jurisdictions
│   ├── Historical separate compliance structures
│   └── Acquisition integration scenarios
│
├── ADVANTAGES
│   ├── Regional expertise utilised
│   ├── Time zone alignment
│   ├── Local language capabilities
│   └── Distributed risk
│
├── DISADVANTAGES
│   ├── Complexity significantly increased
│   ├── Data consistency challenges
│   ├── Coordination overhead
│   ├── Potential gaps or overlaps
│   ├── Multiple systems and processes
│   └── Higher compliance costs
│
├── RISKS
│   ├── Inconsistent data between DFEs
│   ├── Intercompany transactions split across filings
│   ├── Unclear accountability
│   ├── Deadline coordination failures
│   └── Tax authority confusion
│
└── STRUCTURE DIAGRAM

    ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                       UPE                           │

    │                        │                            │
    │        ┌───────────────┼───────────────┐            │

    │   EMEA Region                    APAC Region        │

    │   │ DFE 1   │                    │ DFE 2   │        │
```

## 8.2.4 Group Structure Implications

### Common Group Structures and DFE Selection

```
GROUP STRUCTURE ANALYSIS FOR DFE SELECTION

STRUCTURE 1: Simple Hierarchy
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
        UPE (Implementing Jurisdiction)
              │
    ┌─────────┼─────────┐
    ▼         ▼         ▼
   CE1       CE2       CE3

DFE Selection: UPE is natural choice (no DFE needed - UPE files)
Complexity: Low


STRUCTURE 2: US Parent with Foreign Subsidiaries
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
        UPE (United States - Non-Implementing)
              │
    ┌─────────┼─────────┐
    ▼         ▼         ▼
  UK Sub   DE Sub    FR Sub

DFE Selection: UK Sub commonly chosen (largest, most central)
Complexity: Low-Medium
Key Issue: Must designate DFE; UPE cannot file


STRUCTURE 3: Intermediate Holding Company
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
        UPE (Non-Implementing)
              │
              ▼
        Intermediate HoldCo (Implementing)
              │
    ┌─────────┼─────────┐
    ▼         ▼         ▼
   CE1       CE2       CE3

DFE Selection: Intermediate HoldCo is logical DFE
Complexity: Low
Key Issue: HoldCo may lack operational data access


STRUCTURE 4: Regional Holding Structure
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
              UPE (Non-Implementing)
                    │
        ┌───────────┼───────────┐
        ▼           ▼           ▼
   EMEA HoldCo  APAC HoldCo  Americas HoldCo
        │           │           │
      (subs)      (subs)      (subs)

DFE Selection: Choose one regional HoldCo as single DFE
               OR (not recommended) multiple regional DFEs
Complexity: Medium-High
Key Issue: Coordinate across regions regardless of DFE choice


STRUCTURE 5: PE Structure (Permanent Establishments)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
        UPE (Implementing)
              │
              ▼
        Main Entity
         │    │    │
         PE1  PE2  PE3
        (UK) (DE) (FR)

DFE Selection: Main Entity (PEs are part of same entity)
Complexity: Medium
Key Issue: PE data must be separately identified for GIR


STRUCTURE 6: Joint Venture Involvement
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
        UPE
         │
    ┌────┴────┐
    ▼         ▼
  Sub1    50% JV ──── Other Investor
            │
           JV Sub

DFE Selection: Standard DFE for owned entities
Complexity: High
Key Issue: JV may require separate treatment or coordination
           with other investor's group
```

### Ownership Threshold Considerations

```
Ownership and DFE Eligibility:
│
├── STANDARD CONSTITUENT ENTITIES
│   ├── Entities consolidated in financial statements
│   ├── Generally > 50% ownership (control)
│   ├── Included in single GIR filed by DFE
│   └── No special considerations
│
├── JOINT VENTURES (JVs)
│   ├── 50% or less ownership
│   ├── May be Constituent Entity if equity method
│   ├── Special GIR reporting rules apply
│   ├── May require coordination with JV partner
│   └── DFE still files, but JV section separate
│
├── MINORITY INVESTMENTS
│   ├── Less than equity method threshold
│   ├── Generally not Constituent Entities
│   ├── Not included in GIR
│   └── No DFE implications
│
└── PARTIALLY-OWNED PARENT ENTITIES (POPEs)
    ├── Parent with minority investors
    ├── Still files for its owned subsidiaries
    ├── Ownership percentage affects top-up allocation
    └── DFE can be a POPE
```

## 8.2.5 Special Situations

### Newly Acquired Entities

```
DFE Considerations for Acquisitions:
│
├── DURING ACQUISITION YEAR
│   ├── Acquired entity joins group mid-year
│   ├── May need to update GIR scope
│   ├── DFE must obtain data from acquired entities
│   └── Consider DFE capacity for expanded scope
│
├── POST-ACQUISITION INTEGRATION
│   ├── Acquired group may have had own DFE
│   ├── Need to consolidate into single DFE approach
│   ├── Data systems integration required
│   └── Timeline: Complete before next filing
│
└── ACQUISITION OF ENTITY IN NON-MCAA JURISDICTION
    ├── May affect exchange coverage analysis
    ├── New local filing obligations may arise
    └── Reassess DFE strategy
```

### Restructurings

```
DFE Considerations for Restructurings:
│
├── DFE ENTITY RESTRUCTURED
│   ├── If DFE is sold, merged, or liquidated
│   ├── Must designate new DFE
│   ├── Notify tax authorities as required
│   └── Ensure continuity of filing capability
│
├── GROUP STRUCTURE CHANGES
│   ├── New intermediate holding companies
│   ├── Entity relocations (re-domiciliation)
│   ├── May affect optimal DFE choice
│   └── Review DFE selection annually
│
└── DEMERGERS / SPIN-OFFS
    ├── Resulting groups each need own DFE assessment
    ├── Historical data allocation issues
    └── Coordinate timing of changes
```

### Flow-Through Entities

```
Flow-Through Entity Considerations:
│
├── PARTNERSHIPS / LLCs (Tax Transparent)
│   ├── Generally not Constituent Entities themselves
│   ├── Look through to underlying owners
│   ├── Owners are the Constituent Entities
│   └── DFE selection at owner level
│
├── HYBRID ENTITIES
│   ├── Treated differently in different jurisdictions
│   ├── Follow GloBE location rules
│   ├── May create complexity
│   └── Document treatment carefully
│
└── REVERSE HYBRIDS
    ├── Transparent in parent jurisdiction, opaque locally
    ├── Special GloBE rules apply
    └── Consider in DFE data collection planning
```

## Summary

DFE eligibility assessment requires systematic evaluation of:

1. **Core Criteria:** Constituent Entity status, implementing jurisdiction, not excluded, exchange coverage, practical capacity
2. **Jurisdiction Requirements:** Each jurisdiction may have specific DFE acceptance rules
3. **Single vs. Multiple DFE:** Single DFE strongly preferred for simplicity and consistency
4. **Group Structure:** Ownership chains, holding companies, JVs affect DFE options
5. **Special Situations:** Acquisitions, restructurings, flow-through entities

Use the DFE Eligibility Flowchart and Assessment Template to document your analysis systematically.

## Section Review Questions

1. What are the five core eligibility criteria for a DFE?

2. Why is a single DFE structure generally preferred over multiple DFEs?

3. Can an Excluded Entity (such as a pension fund) serve as a DFE?

4. What should you verify regarding exchange agreements before selecting a DFE jurisdiction?

5. How does a US-parented group structure affect DFE requirements?

**Answers:** 1) Constituent Entity status, implementing jurisdiction, not excluded, exchange coverage, practical capacity; 2) Simplicity, data consistency, clear accountability, lower costs, clearer audit trail; 3) No, Excluded Entities cannot serve as DFE; 4) Verify the jurisdiction is a GIR MCAA signatory with exchange agreements covering other group jurisdictions; 5) DFE is mandatory because the US has not implemented Pillar Two, so UPE cannot file GIR.
