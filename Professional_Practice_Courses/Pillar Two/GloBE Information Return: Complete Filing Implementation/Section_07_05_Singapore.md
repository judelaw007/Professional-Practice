# Section 7.5: Singapore

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this section, you will be able to:
- Navigate the myTax Portal for Pillar Two filings using Corppass
- Register in-scope MNE groups with IRAS
- Designate the appropriate filing entities (DFE and GFE)
- Submit the GloBE Information Return and MTT Return
- Understand Singapore's DTT and MTT framework
- Apply the Singapore Pillar Two Filing Checklist for compliance assurance

## Introduction

Singapore implemented Pillar Two through the Multinational Enterprise (Minimum Tax) Act 2024, effective for financial years beginning on or after 1 January 2025. The legislation introduces:

- **Domestic Top-up Tax (DTT)** - Ensures 15% minimum ETR on Singapore profits
- **Multinational Enterprise Top-up Tax (MTT)** - Singapore's Income Inclusion Rule (IIR)
- **UTPR** - Not yet implemented; to be considered at a later stage

**Critical Point:** Singapore-parented MNE groups face both DTT and MTT obligations. The first GIR and MTT Return filings are due by 30 June 2027 (18 months after FY 2025 year-end for calendar year groups). Registration deadline for 2025 in-scope entities is **30 September 2025**.

## 7.5.1 Singapore Pillar Two Framework

### Legislative Framework

```
Singapore Pillar Two Legislation:
│
├── PRIMARY LEGISLATION
│   ├── Multinational Enterprise (Minimum Tax) Act 2024
│   ├── Gazetted: 27 November 2024
│   ├── Effective: 1 January 2025
│   └── Act No. 36 of 2024
│
├── SUPPORTING REGULATIONS
│   ├── Multinational Enterprise (Minimum Tax) Regulations 2024
│   ├── Gazetted: 30 December 2024
│   └── Effective: 1 January 2025
│
└── RELATED AMENDMENTS
    └── Income Tax (Amendment) Act 2024 (Act No. 35 of 2024)
```

### Scope

```
Singapore Pillar Two Scope:
│
├── REVENUE THRESHOLD
│   ├── €750 million annual group revenue
│   ├── In at least 2 of the 4 preceding financial years
│   └── Based on UPE consolidated financial statements
│
├── CHARGING MECHANISMS
│   ├── DTT: Effective FY starting on/after 1 January 2025
│   ├── MTT (IIR): Effective FY starting on/after 1 January 2025
│   └── UTPR: Not yet implemented
│
├── SINGAPORE NEXUS
│   ├── Singapore-parented MNE groups → MTT applies
│   ├── Singapore constituent entities → DTT applies
│   └── Singapore PE of foreign entity → DTT applies
│
└── EXAMPLE: FY 2025 APPLICATION
    └── Revenue threshold tested against FY 2021-2024
```

### Key Singapore Terminology

| Term | Acronym | Description |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Multinational Enterprise Top-up Tax | MTT | Singapore's IIR |
| Domestic Top-up Tax | DTT | Singapore's QDMTT equivalent |
| Designated local DTT Filing Entity | DFE | Entity filing DTT return |
| Designated local GloBE Information Return Filing Entity | GFE | Entity filing GIR |
| Comptroller | - | IRAS Comptroller of Income Tax |

## 7.5.2 Registration Requirements

### Who Must Register

```
Singapore Registration Requirements:
│
├── OBLIGATION
│   ├── Ultimate Parent Entity (UPE) of in-scope MNE group
│   ├── Must notify Comptroller of liability to be registered
│   └── Applies to all in-scope groups with Singapore presence
│
├── REGISTRATION DEADLINE
│   ├── For FY 2025: 30 September 2025
│   └── Constituent Entities and Joint Ventures included
│
├── COMPTROLLER POWERS
│   ├── May register groups that fail to self-register
│   └── Penalties for failure to register
│
└── DESIGNATED ENTITIES
    ├── DFE: Designated for DTT filing
    ├── GFE: Designated for GIR filing
    └── Must be Singapore-located constituent entity
```

### Designation of Filing Entities

```
Filing Entity Designation:
│
├── DESIGNATED LOCAL DTT FILING ENTITY (DFE)
│   ├── Designated by: UPE of in-scope MNE group
│   ├── Must be: Constituent entity located in Singapore
│   ├── Responsibility: File DTT return, pay DTT
│   └── Joint liability: Other Singapore CEs if DFE fails to pay
│
└── DESIGNATED LOCAL GIR FILING ENTITY (GFE)
    ├── Designated by: Constituent entities of MNE group
    ├── Must be: Constituent entity located in Singapore
    ├── Responsibility: File GIR or notification
    └── MTT liability: Parent entities in Singapore
```

## 7.5.3 Filing Obligations Overview

### Three Filing Components

```
Singapore Pillar Two Filing Obligations:
│
├── 1. GloBE INFORMATION RETURN (GIR)
│   ├── Filed by: GFE
│   ├── Filed to: IRAS (Comptroller)
│   ├── Deadline: 15 months after FY end (18 months first year)
│   ├── First deadline: 30 June 2027 (for FY ending 31 Dec 2025)
│   ├── Alternative: Notification if GIR filed elsewhere
│   └── Content: Full OECD GIR specification
│
├── 2. MTT RETURN (for Singapore-parented groups)
│   ├── Filed by: Parent entities in Singapore liable to MTT
│   ├── Filed to: IRAS (Comptroller)
│   ├── Deadline: 15 months after FY end (18 months first year)
│   ├── First deadline: 30 June 2027 (for FY ending 31 Dec 2025)
│   └── Content: MTT liability calculation and payment details
│
└── 3. DTT RETURN (for Singapore low-taxed entities)
    ├── Filed by: DFE
    ├── Filed to: IRAS (Comptroller)
    ├── Deadline: Per DTT filing requirements
    ├── Content: DTT liability for Singapore low-taxed profits
    └── Joint liability: Other Singapore CEs
```

### Deadline Summary

| Filing | Standard | First Year | First FY 2025 Date |
|--------|----------|------------|-------------------|
| Registration | As required | 30 Sep 2025 | 30 September 2025 |
| GIR | 15 months | 18 months | 30 June 2027 |
| MTT Return | 15 months | 18 months | 30 June 2027 |
| MTT Payment | Return + 1 month | Return + 1 month | 31 July 2027 |
| DTT Return | Per requirements | Per requirements | TBC |

### GIR Notification (When Filed Elsewhere)

```
GIR Notification Process:
│
├── WHEN REQUIRED
│   ├── GIR filed in another jurisdiction
│   ├── By another filing entity of the MNE group
│   └── Singapore does not need to file full GIR
│
├── CONTENT
│   ├── Particulars of the filing entity
│   ├── Jurisdiction where GIR filed
│   └── Singapore GFE identification
│
└── DEADLINE
    └── Same as GIR deadline (15/18 months)
```

## 7.5.5 GloBE Information Return Filing

### Filing Process

```
Singapore GIR Filing Process:
│
├── PREPARATION
│   ├── Complete GIR data compilation per OECD specification
│   ├── Include all constituent entities worldwide
│   ├── Calculate ETR and top-up tax by jurisdiction
│   ├── Document elections and safe harbour claims
│   └── Generate XML file per OECD Schema v1.0
│
├── VALIDATION
│   ├── Perform three-tier validation
│   ├── Tier 1: Well-formedness
│   ├── Tier 2: Schema validation
│   ├── Tier 3: Business rules
│   └── Obtain internal sign-off
│
├── SUBMISSION
│   ├── GFE logs in to myTax Portal via Corppass
│   ├── Navigate to GIR submission section
│   ├── Upload validated XML file
│   ├── Complete any additional declarations
│   └── Submit and receive confirmation
│
└── POST-SUBMISSION
    ├── Download confirmation
    ├── Archive submission records
    └── Prepare MTT Return if applicable
```

### GIR Content Requirements

The GIR follows the OECD specification and includes:

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| General Information | UPE details, group structure, filing entity |
| Constituent Entities | All CEs worldwide with identification |
| Corporate Structure | Ownership chains and percentages |
| ETR Calculations | By jurisdiction per GloBE rules |
| Top-up Tax | Calculations and allocations |
| Elections | All elections made for the period |

## 7.5.6 MTT Return Filing

### When MTT Applies

```
Singapore MTT (IIR) Application:
│
├── APPLIES TO
│   ├── Singapore-parented MNE groups
│   ├── Parent entities located in Singapore
│   └── Low-taxed foreign constituent entities
│
├── MTT LIABILITY
│   ├── Top-up tax on foreign low-taxed entities
│   ├── Where foreign jurisdiction ETR < 15%
│   ├── Singapore parent "includes" low-taxed income
│   └── Calculated per GloBE IIR rules
│
└── NOT APPLICABLE IF
    ├── No Singapore parent entity
    ├── All foreign entities meet 15% ETR
    └── Safe harbour provisions apply
```

### MTT Return Process

```
MTT Return Filing:
│
├── DEADLINE
│   ├── 15 months after financial year end
│   ├── 18 months for first (transition) year
│   └── First deadline: 30 June 2027 (FY 2025)
│
├── CONTENT
│   ├── MTT liability calculation
│   ├── Identification of low-taxed foreign jurisdictions
│   ├── Top-up tax amounts by jurisdiction
│   └── Supporting documentation references
│
├── PAYMENT
│   ├── Due: 1 month after return deadline
│   ├── First payment: 31 July 2027
│   └── Payment via IRAS approved methods
│
└── FILING PROCESS
    ├── Access myTax Portal
    ├── Navigate to MTT Return section
    ├── Complete return
    ├── Submit and receive confirmation
    └── Arrange payment
```

## 7.5.7 Domestic Top-up Tax (DTT)

### Singapore DTT Framework

```
Singapore DTT:
│
├── PURPOSE
│   ├── Ensures 15% minimum ETR on Singapore profits
│   ├── Applies before foreign IIR can collect
│   ├── Retains top-up tax revenue in Singapore
│   └── Similar to QDMTT in other jurisdictions
│
├── WHEN APPLICABLE
│   ├── Singapore jurisdictional ETR < 15%
│   ├── May occur with significant tax incentives
│   ├── Pioneer status, development incentives
│   └── Intellectual property incentives
│
├── CALCULATION
│   ├── Based on GloBE income for Singapore
│   ├── GloBE covered taxes in Singapore
│   ├── Substance-based income exclusions
│   └── Top-up to reach 15% ETR
│
└── FILING
    ├── DFE files DTT return
    ├── Payment per IRAS requirements
    └── Joint liability for other Singapore CEs
```

### Singapore Tax Rate Context

```
Singapore Corporate Tax Rates:
│
├── STANDARD RATE: 17%
│   └── Generally above 15% minimum
│
├── TAX INCENTIVES (may reduce ETR below 15%)
│   ├── Pioneer Certificate: 0-5%
│   ├── Development and Expansion Incentive: 5-10%
│   ├── IP Development Incentive: 5-10%
│   ├── Finance and Treasury Centre: 8%
│   └── Aircraft Leasing Scheme: 5-8%
│
└── DTT IMPACT
    ├── DTT "tops up" incentive-reduced ETR to 15%
    ├── Reduces value of incentives for large MNEs
    └── Groups should model impact on tax planning
```

## 7.5.8 Penalties and Compliance

### Penalty Framework

```
Singapore Pillar Two Penalties:
│
├── FAILURE TO REGISTER
│   ├── Comptroller may register group directly
│   └── Penalties per Act provisions
│
├── LATE FILING
│   ├── GIR: Penalties for late/non-filing
│   ├── MTT Return: Penalties apply
│   └── Specifics per IRAS guidance
│
├── LATE PAYMENT
│   ├── MTT payment due 1 month after return
│   ├── Late payment penalties apply
│   └── Interest on outstanding amounts
│
├── INCORRECT RETURNS
│   ├── Penalties for incorrect information
│   ├── May include additional tax
│   └── Up to 200% of undercharged tax (general rule)
│
└── TRANSITIONAL RELIEF
    ├── OECD recommends penalty relief in early years
    ├── Reasonable efforts considered
    └── Check IRAS guidance for specifics
```

### Joint and Several Liability

```
DTT Joint Liability:
│
├── PRIMARY LIABILITY
│   └── DFE responsible for DTT payment
│
└── SECONDARY LIABILITY
    ├── If DFE fails to pay DTT
    ├── Other Singapore CEs jointly liable
    └── Comptroller may collect from any Singapore CE
```

## Summary

Singapore's implementation through the Multinational Enterprise (Minimum Tax) Act 2024 creates comprehensive obligations for in-scope MNE groups:

1. **Registration** - By 30 September 2025 for FY 2025 in-scope entities
2. **GIR Filing** - By GFE within 15/18 months
3. **MTT Return** - For Singapore-parented groups, due 15/18 months after FY end
4. **MTT Payment** - Due 1 month after return deadline
5. **DTT** - Applies if Singapore ETR below 15%

Key considerations:
- Corppass required for myTax Portal access
- UTPR not yet implemented
- Tax incentives may trigger DTT liability
- Joint and several liability for DTT across Singapore CEs
