# Section 8.1: DFE Concept and Requirements

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this section, you will be able to:
- Understand the role and purpose of a Designated Filing Entity (DFE)
- Identify when DFE election is required versus optional
- Evaluate the benefits and responsibilities of DFE status
- Determine the appropriate DFE for your MNE group structure
- Apply the DFE decision framework to common scenarios

## Introduction

The Designated Filing Entity (DFE) is a central concept in Pillar Two compliance, enabling MNE groups to streamline their GIR filing obligations through a single, coordinated approach. Rather than requiring every constituent entity to file separately, the GloBE Rules allow groups to designate one entity to file the GIR on behalf of the entire group.

This section explains the DFE concept, when designation is required or beneficial, and the key considerations for selecting the appropriate DFE.

---

## 8.1.1 What is a Designated Filing Entity?

### Definition

A Designated Filing Entity (DFE) is a constituent entity within an MNE group that is appointed to file the GloBE Information Return (GIR) on behalf of all constituent entities in the group.

```
Designated Filing Entity (DFE):
│
├── DEFINITION
│   ├── A constituent entity designated by the MNE group
│   ├── To file the GIR with a tax authority
│   ├── On behalf of all constituent entities
│   └── In lieu of each entity filing separately
│
├── LEGAL BASIS
│   ├── OECD Model Rules Article 8.1.3
│   ├── EU Minimum Tax Directive Article 44
│   ├── Implemented in domestic legislation
│   └── Supported by GIR MCAA exchange framework
│
├── KEY CHARACTERISTICS
│   ├── Single point of filing for the group
│   ├── Responsible for GIR accuracy and completeness
│   ├── Coordinates data collection from all CEs
│   └── Interfaces with tax authority on GIR matters
│
└── RELATIONSHIP TO UPE
    ├── UPE is the default filing entity in many jurisdictions
    ├── DFE is an alternative when UPE cannot file
    ├── DFE takes on UPE's filing responsibilities
    └── DFE status does not change ownership structure
```

### DFE vs. UPE Filing

```
Filing Entity Comparison:
│
├── ULTIMATE PARENT ENTITY (UPE) FILING
│   ├── Default approach under GloBE Rules
│   ├── UPE files GIR in its jurisdiction of residence
│   ├── Works when UPE jurisdiction has implemented Pillar Two
│   ├── Works when UPE jurisdiction has GIR MCAA
│   └── Simplest approach for most groups
│
├── DESIGNATED FILING ENTITY (DFE) FILING
│   ├── Alternative when UPE cannot effectively file
│   ├── DFE located in implementing jurisdiction
│   ├── DFE has exchange agreements with other jurisdictions
│   ├── DFE takes on coordination responsibility
│   └── Required in certain circumstances
│
└── KEY DIFFERENCES
    │
    ├── UPE Filing                    │ DFE Filing
    ├─────────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────
    │ Automatic under rules           │ Requires designation
    │ UPE jurisdiction must implement │ DFE jurisdiction implements
    │ No election needed              │ Election/notification needed
    │ Natural coordination point      │ May require extra coordination
    └─────────────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────
```

## 8.1.2 When is DFE Election Required?

### Mandatory DFE Scenarios

```
Situations Requiring DFE Election:
│
├── SCENARIO 1: UPE Jurisdiction Has Not Implemented Pillar Two
│   │
│   ├── Example: US-parented MNE group
│   │   └── United States has not implemented Pillar Two
│   │
│   ├── Impact: UPE cannot file GIR in US
│   │
│   └── Solution: Designate DFE in implementing jurisdiction
│       └── E.g., UK, Germany, or Netherlands subsidiary
│
├── SCENARIO 2: UPE Jurisdiction Has No GIR Exchange Agreement
│   │
│   ├── UPE jurisdiction implemented Pillar Two but
│   │   no QCAA with jurisdictions where group operates
│   │
│   ├── Impact: Central filing would not satisfy other jurisdictions
│   │
│   └── Solution: DFE in jurisdiction with broader exchange network
│
├── SCENARIO 3: Local Law Requires DFE Designation
│   │
│   ├── Some jurisdictions require local DFE designation
│   │   regardless of UPE filing
│   │
│   ├── Examples:
│   │   ├── Singapore: Designated Local GIR Filing Entity (GFE)
│   │   ├── Australia: Designated Filing Entity concept
│   │   └── Other jurisdictions with local requirements
│   │
│   └── Solution: Comply with local designation requirements
│
└── SCENARIO 4: UPE is an Excluded Entity
    │
    ├── Certain entities excluded from GloBE Rules
    │   (government entities, pension funds, etc.)
    │
    ├── Excluded entity cannot fulfil filing obligations
    │
    └── Solution: Designate non-excluded CE as DFE
```

### Common Trigger: US-Parented Groups

The most common scenario requiring DFE election involves US-parented MNE groups:

```
US-Parented MNE Group DFE Requirement:
│
├── SITUATION
│   ├── Ultimate Parent Entity located in United States
│   ├── US has not implemented Pillar Two
│   ├── No US GIR filing mechanism available
│   └── Subsidiaries in Pillar Two implementing jurisdictions
│
├── REQUIREMENT
│   ├── Must designate a DFE in an implementing jurisdiction
│   ├── DFE files GIR with its local tax authority
│   ├── GIR exchanged with other jurisdictions via MCAA
│   └── Local notifications filed in non-DFE jurisdictions
│
├── TYPICAL DFE CHOICES
│   ├── UK holding company or operating entity
│   ├── Netherlands holding company
│   ├── Irish holding company
│   ├── German operating company
│   └── Based on group structure and operations
│
└── CONSIDERATIONS
    ├── DFE jurisdiction should have broad exchange network
    ├── DFE should have capacity to coordinate group-wide
    ├── Consider existing compliance infrastructure
    └── Ensure DFE has access to all required data
```

## 8.1.3 When is DFE Election Optional?

### Optional DFE Scenarios

```
Situations Where DFE is Optional:
│
├── SCENARIO 1: UPE Jurisdiction Implements Pillar Two
│   │
│   ├── UPE can file GIR in its jurisdiction
│   ├── Exchange agreements in place
│   ├── No need for DFE designation
│   └── UPE filing is the default
│
├── SCENARIO 2: Operational Efficiency Choice
│   │
│   ├── UPE could file, but group prefers different jurisdiction
│   ├── May be due to:
│   │   ├── Language preferences
│   │   ├── Existing compliance infrastructure
│   │   ├── Tax team location
│   │   └── Adviser relationships
│   │
│   └── Optional DFE designation for convenience
│
└── SCENARIO 3: Multiple Implementing Jurisdictions
    │
    ├── Group could file in any of several jurisdictions
    ├── All have Pillar Two implementation
    ├── All participate in GIR MCAA
    │
    └── Strategic choice of filing jurisdiction
```

### When NOT to Use DFE

```
Situations Where UPE Filing is Preferred:
│
├── UPE jurisdiction has implemented Pillar Two
├── UPE jurisdiction participates in GIR MCAA
├── UPE has natural access to all group data
├── Group tax function based at UPE location
├── Existing UPE compliance infrastructure
│
└── In these cases, UPE filing is simpler and more efficient
```

## 8.1.4 Benefits of DFE Status

### Advantages for the Group

```
Benefits of DFE Approach:
│
├── COMPLIANCE EFFICIENCY
│   ├── Single filing point for entire group
│   ├── Consistent data across all filings
│   ├── Centralised quality control
│   ├── Reduced duplication of effort
│   └── Clear accountability
│
├── REDUCED FILING BURDEN
│   ├── Other jurisdictions receive GIR via exchange
│   ├── Local entities only file notifications
│   ├── Notifications much simpler than full GIR
│   └── Significant time and cost savings
│
├── DATA CONSISTENCY
│   ├── One master GIR dataset
│   ├── All jurisdictions receive same information
│   ├── Reduces reconciliation issues
│   └── Clearer audit trail
│
├── COORDINATION BENEFITS
│   ├── Single point of contact for GIR queries
│   ├── Easier to manage amendments
│   ├── Streamlined communication with authorities
│   └── Clearer governance structure
│
└── STRATEGIC FLEXIBILITY
    ├── Choose optimal filing jurisdiction
    ├── Leverage best compliance infrastructure
    ├── Align with group tax function location
    └── Manage language and portal preferences
```

### Benefits for Tax Authorities

```
Tax Authority Benefits:
│
├── Consistent information across jurisdictions
├── Reduced duplicate filings to process
├── Standardised exchange format
└── Clearer source of information
```

## 8.1.5 Responsibilities of DFE Status

### Core DFE Responsibilities

```
DFE Responsibilities:
│
├── GIR PREPARATION
│   ├── Compile data from ALL constituent entities worldwide
│   ├── Perform GloBE calculations per OECD specification
│   ├── Determine ETR by jurisdiction
│   ├── Calculate top-up tax allocations
│   ├── Document elections and safe harbour claims
│   └── Generate compliant XML file
│
├── GIR FILING
│   ├── Register with local tax authority (if required)
│   ├── Submit GIR by applicable deadline
│   ├── Obtain and retain filing confirmation
│   ├── Respond to authority queries
│   └── File amendments if corrections needed
│
├── COORDINATION
│   ├── Communicate filing to other group entities
│   ├── Provide notification details for local filings
│   ├── Coordinate with local entities on domestic returns
│   ├── Ensure consistency across all filings
│   └── Manage timeline dependencies
│
├── DATA MANAGEMENT
│   ├── Establish data collection processes
│   ├── Ensure data quality and completeness
│   ├── Maintain supporting documentation
│   ├── Implement version control
│   └── Archive records per retention requirements
│
└── ONGOING OBLIGATIONS
    ├── Annual GIR filing for each fiscal year
    ├── Monitor regulatory changes
    ├── Update elections as needed
    ├── Manage group-wide compliance calendar
    └── Report status to group leadership
```

### Liability Considerations

```
DFE Liability Framework:
│
├── FILING RESPONSIBILITY
│   ├── DFE is responsible for timely, accurate filing
│   ├── Penalties for late/incorrect filing apply to DFE
│   ├── DFE jurisdiction's penalty rules apply
│   └── May be able to seek recovery from other CEs
│
├── DATA RESPONSIBILITY
│   ├── DFE relies on data from other CEs
│   ├── DFE should implement data quality controls
│   ├── Intra-group agreements may allocate data responsibility
│   └── Cannot delegate DFE's ultimate responsibility
│
├── JOINT LIABILITY (varies by jurisdiction)
│   ├── Some jurisdictions impose joint liability
│   ├── Local CEs may be liable if DFE fails
│   ├── Check local rules in each jurisdiction
│   └── Consider intra-group indemnities
│
└── RISK MITIGATION
    ├── Clear data provision agreements with CEs
    ├── Documented sign-off procedures
    ├── Insurance/indemnity considerations
    └── Escalation procedures for data issues
```

## 8.1.6 DFE Decision Framework

### Decision Tree

```
DFE DECISION FRAMEWORK

START: Does your UPE jurisdiction implement Pillar Two?
│
├── NO
│   │
│   └── DFE REQUIRED
│       │
│       └── Which jurisdiction should be DFE?
│           ├── Consider jurisdictions where group has significant presence
│           ├── Evaluate GIR MCAA participation
│           ├── Assess compliance infrastructure
│           └── SELECT DFE JURISDICTION → Proceed to DFE Election
│
└── YES
    │
    └── Does UPE jurisdiction have GIR exchange agreements
        with all jurisdictions where group operates?
        │
        ├── NO (Limited Exchange Network)
        │   │
        │   └── Consider DFE in jurisdiction with broader exchange
        │       │
        │       └── Evaluate: Is operational benefit worth complexity?
        │           ├── YES → Designate DFE
        │           └── NO → Use UPE filing + separate local filings
        │
        └── YES (Full Exchange Network)
            │
            └── UPE FILING IS DEFAULT (No DFE needed)
                │
                └── Optional: Consider DFE for operational reasons?
                    ├── Language/portal preferences
                    ├── Tax team location
                    ├── Adviser relationships
                    │
                    ├── YES to any → Consider optional DFE
                    └── NO → Use UPE as filing entity
```

## Summary

The Designated Filing Entity (DFE) enables MNE groups to streamline Pillar Two compliance through centralised filing:

1. **DFE Purpose:** Single filing point for the group, reducing duplicate filings
2. **When Required:** UPE jurisdiction hasn't implemented Pillar Two (e.g., US-parented groups)
3. **When Optional:** UPE can file, but group prefers different jurisdiction
4. **Key Benefits:** Compliance efficiency, data consistency, reduced filing burden
5. **Key Responsibilities:** Data collection, GIR preparation, filing, coordination
6. **Selection Criteria:** Implementation status, MCAA participation, infrastructure, expertise

The DFE Assessment Worksheet provides a structured approach to determining whether DFE designation is needed and selecting the optimal entity.
